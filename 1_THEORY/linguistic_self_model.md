# Linguistic Self Model

## Abstract
The Linguistic Self Model (LSM) proposes that the self is not a static entity but an emergent construct maintained through continuous linguistic self-reference.

## Core Claim
The self exists *because it is described*, updated, and reinforced through language.

## Components
- **Narrative Loop**: continuous self-description
- **Reference Tokens**: pronouns, names, roles
- **Temporal Binding**: language stitching past and future selves

## Formal Sketch
Let \( L_t \) be the linguistic state at time \( t \).  
The self \( S \) is defined as:
\[
S = \lim_{t \to \infty} f(L_t)
\]
where \( f \) preserves identity coherence under transformation.
